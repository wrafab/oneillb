Vocabulary, Charts, and More!

capacitor: an electrical used to store energy temporarily

transistor: a semi-conducter used to amplify or switch electronic symbols

resistor: a device having a designed resistance to the passage of an electric current

led: a light-emitting diode diode: a semi-conductor with only one flow

potentiometer: a meter that measures the amount of light

breadboard: a place where most electronics are hooked into

multimeter: an electronic measuring device used to check the voltage, current, and resistance

switch: a device the connects or disconnects the connection in a circuit



Ohm's Law is Voltage = Resistance * Current. To measure the amount of resistance on a resistor, add the first two colors and multiply by the third. The fourth band is the accuracy (room for failure) of the resistor.


Electronic Exercises.
