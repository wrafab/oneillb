Week Two!


Autodesk Fusion 360

Getting the hang of Autodesk was kind of hard at first, but I eventually got it. Learning how to extrude was a bit harder. I feel like sketcing with a tablet and a pencil would end up being easier than using a mouse. My hand felt shaky while drawing rectangles and such. A lot of what I did didn't end up working so well, especially when I was trying to flip between axes. I started sketching a bit more of my final project. Overall, Autodesk Fusion 360 worked well!


What I made.

I made a few different designs. Six of them were given to me from my teacher, Mr. Gerber, and one of the other ones was a hinge. The hinge was probably one of the hardest things to do. Extruding the different parts was good warmup for my other designs, but it ended up taking a bunch of time. I wasn't able to print it, but will soon!

This was the first challenge we had to make during class. We were given about 30 minutes to follow the exact drawn design and translate it into AutoDesk Fusion 360.

This was the hinge I made on Fusion 360. I spent a majority of my time on it, which made it harder to do my other designs below. I used a Youtube Tutorial Video on How To Make a Hinge in Fusion 360 which I found to be very helpful.

Some of the designs were harder to make than others, but I still got through with most of them. After I finished making them, I felt much more well-versed in using Fusion 360.
